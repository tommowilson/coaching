Feldenkrais

Reconnecting with the quiet intelligence of your body.

What this work is about

Most of us learned to move — and to live — by adapting. We tensed where we needed to protect. We pushed through when we should have paused. We forgot how effortless movement can feel.

The Feldenkrais Method offers a way back. It’s not exercise, and it’s not about fixing what’s “wrong.” It’s a process of learning — through awareness — how to move with more freedom, sensitivity, and choice.

As you learn to move differently, you start to live differently. Your movement, emotions, and thinking are all expressions of the same system. When one changes, the rest follows.

How it helps

People come to Feldenkrais for many reasons — pain, tension, injury, anxiety, or simply curiosity — but what they often find is something deeper:

  • A lighter, more fluid sense of movement
  • Less pain and strain
  • A calmer, more settled nervous system
  • A clearer sense of self in space and in life
  • Renewed creativity and ease

It’s not about stretching or strengthening. It’s about rediscovering your body’s natural intelligence — the one that’s been there all along.

What happens in a session

There are two main ways to experience Feldenkrais:

Awareness Through Movement — gentle guided explorations done through verbal instruction, usually lying or sitting. You’re invited to notice how you move, sense differences, and discover easier options.

Functional Integration — hands-on, non-invasive sessions where I move you gently to help your system feel new patterns of coordination and ease.

Each session is unique. There’s no right or wrong way — only curiosity, attention, and discovery.

Is this for you?

Feldenkrais might be for you if:

  • You’re dealing with pain or tension that hasn’t shifted with other methods
  • You’re interested in moving with more freedom and pleasure
  • You’re curious about the connection between movement, emotion, and awareness

If you’re new to it, you can start with a single session or a small series. No experience required — just curiosity.
